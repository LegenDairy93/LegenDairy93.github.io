How I build

Reliability is a product feature.

The interesting work is not hiding uncertainty. It is designing systems that expose it clearly and respond safely.

01

Ground the output

Connect claims to evidence, inputs, and execution history so a person can verify what happened.

02

Validate boundaries

Use schemas and explicit contracts to contain malformed or invented output before it travels downstream.

03

Show the path

For an agent, tool calls and intermediate decisions matter as much as the final sentence.

04

Design for constraints

Offline, local-first, CPU-only, and human review are deliberate architecture choices—not footnotes.

05

Measure honestly

Publish evaluation results, failure cases, and limitations. A believable project includes what did not work.

06

Make it legible

A technical system earns adoption when its value and its behavior are understandable outside the codebase.
